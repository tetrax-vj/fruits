Banana

It was primarily cultivated in Papua New Guinea and parts of southeastern Asia. Cultivation of the original banana is believed to pre-date rice.

Coconut

Sailors from Portugal found it in India first, and they named it coconut because the brown hair of the fruit reminded them of a witch called Coco.

Mango

Mangos orginated in Southeast Asia and India, where references to the fruit are documented in Hindu writings dating back to 4000 B.C.

Fig

originated in northern Asia Minor and spread with the Greeks and the Romans throughout the Mediterranean region.

Avacado

Avocado, also called alligator pear, native to the Western Hemisphere from Mexico south to the Andean regions

Black Berries

blackberries are particularly abundant in eastern North America and on the Pacific coast of that continent and are cultivated in many areas of North America and Europe.

Pears

The pear's origin and domestication at two different points, China and Asia Minor until the Middle East.

Straw Berries

Strawberries are native to North America. The first colonists in America shipped the native larger strawberry plants back to Europe as early as 1600.
